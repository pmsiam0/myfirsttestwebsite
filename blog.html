A Perfectly Ordinary Tuesday

March 31, 2026

I woke up this morning to the sound of birds outside my window. It was earlier than usual, around 6 AM, and the sunlight was already sneaking through the curtains. I lay there for a few minutes just listening — it felt like a good start to the day.

Breakfast was simple: a bowl of oatmeal with some sliced banana and a cup of black coffee. Nothing fancy, but it hit the spot. I ate at the kitchen table while scrolling through the morning news, trying to catch up on what happened overnight.

The afternoon was spent mostly working from home. Between meetings I managed to squeeze in a short walk around the neighborhood. The air was fresh, and a few neighbors waved hello as I passed by. Those small moments make a big difference.

In the evening I cooked a simple pasta dinner, watched an episode of a show I've been slowly working through, and called my mom for about twenty minutes. She's doing well. Before bed I read a few pages of my book and called it a night around 10:30.

Not every day needs to be extraordinary. Some days just being present and getting through the small routines is enough — and honestly, that feels pretty good.
